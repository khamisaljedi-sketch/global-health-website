Ahmed Mokhallalati

MBChB ABHS(Plast) FRCS(Eng) ad eundem

I'm a plastic and reconstructive surgeon at Hull University Teaching Hospitals. Breast reconstruction, limb reconstruction, skin cancer, hand surgery — that's what I spend most of my time doing. I also cover trauma and emergency plastic surgery, which means the work is rarely predictable.

I did my fellowship training in London — advanced microsurgery at Guy's and St Thomas', with time at King's College Hospital as well. The microsurgery piece matters a lot to me because it opens up options for patients that you simply don't have otherwise.

Outside the NHS, I've spent time working in Gaza — managing trauma, burns and blast injuries in conditions that are, to put it mildly, very different from what I'm used to in Hull. That work has changed how I think about surgery, about what's actually essential, and about what good care looks like when the system around you is broken.

Initiative

Global MedReach Initiative

I founded Global MedReach because I kept seeing the same problem: visiting surgeons would arrive, operate for a few weeks, and leave — and six months later nothing had changed for the patients who needed ongoing care. The initiative is an attempt to do something more useful than that.

The focus is on training local surgeons, building proper governance frameworks, and helping develop services that can actually function independently. It's slower and less photogenic than flying in to do operations. But it's more likely to matter in the long run.
