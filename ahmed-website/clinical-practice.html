What I treat

Clinical Practice

Plastic surgery covers more ground than most people expect. Here's what I actually do.

Reconstructive Surgery

Trauma & Limb Reconstruction

This is the work I find most demanding — and most rewarding. Patients who've had a serious injury, a wound that won't close, or a previous operation that didn't go as planned. When the straightforward options don't work, microsurgery gives us routes that simply weren't available a generation ago. Free flaps, perforator flaps, tissue transfer — not because they're impressive, but because sometimes they're the only thing that saves a limb.

I work closely with orthopaedic colleagues on bone and joint infections. These cases need both specialties involved from the start — not sequentially, but together. That collaboration makes a real difference to outcomes.

Breast Surgery

Breast Reconstruction & Aesthetic Surgery

Breast reconstruction after mastectomy is one of those decisions that patients often feel rushed through. I try not to rush it. The options — implant-based, using your own tissue, immediate or delayed — each have real trade-offs, and the right answer depends on your body, your life, and what matters most to you. I'll explain all of it properly before we decide anything.

I also do aesthetic breast surgery — augmentation, reduction, uplift. The same principle applies: we talk first, thoroughly, and surgery only happens when I think it's the right thing for you.

Oncology

Skin Cancer Surgery

Most skin cancers, caught and treated properly, are very manageable. The key words there are "properly" and "treated." I work within multidisciplinary teams, which means every case gets reviewed before we operate — not just by me, but by a team. Complete clearance comes first. Then reconstruction that leaves you looking and functioning as well as possible.

I manage BCCs, SCCs, melanoma and rarer skin tumours. If you've been given a diagnosis and aren't sure what happens next, come and talk to me.

Specialist Procedures

Complex Wounds, Lymphoedema & Body Contouring

Complex wounds — the kind that have been going on for months, that have already had multiple procedures, that have become infected or won't granulate — are something I see a fair amount of. There's usually still something that can be done, but it requires a proper assessment first, not just another dressing change.

For lymphoedema, I perform LVA (lymphovenous anastomosis) and vascularised lymph node transfer. These procedures can make a significant difference for the right patients. I'll tell you honestly if I think you're one of them — or if I think conservative management is still worth pursuing first.

Body contouring after major weight loss is something I find genuinely worthwhile. People who've done the work to change their health and are left with excess skin that affects how they move, exercise, and feel about themselves — that's a real problem, not a cosmetic indulgence. Surgery can help, and I think it often should.

Aesthetic Surgery

Cosmetic Surgery of the Face, Breast & Body

I do cosmetic surgery and I take it as seriously as any other part of my practice. How someone feels about how they look isn't trivial — it shapes confidence, relationships, how people move through the world. When surgery can genuinely help with that, it's worth doing well.

What I won't do is tell someone what they want to hear if I don't believe the operation will deliver it. I've had consultations where I've said "I don't think this will give you what you're hoping for" and recommended something different, or nothing at all. That's not common, but it happens — and I'd rather have that conversation than not.
